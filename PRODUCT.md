# Artificial Intelligencers

register: brand

## What this is

The web home of the Artificial Intelligencers, a monthly Nashville meetup for
anyone, beginner to expert, exploring building with AI. The site's one job:
get people to the next meetup. Event details are the primary content; the
brand is the hook that makes a stranger want to show up.

**Artificial Intelligencers, n.** — any beginners to experts who are willing to
both share their personal or professional journeys learning, experimenting,
developing, and applying Artificial Intelligence in any form, and support
others doing the same.

## Users

- Nashville developers, data folks, students, and the AI-curious who found a
  link in Slack, Meetup, or a friend's text, deciding whether Wednesday night
  is worth leaving the house for.
- Returning members checking the date, time, and venue (it rotates:
  breweries, libraries, sponsor offices).
- Potential presenters and sponsors sizing up whether this group is legit.

## Brand voice

A learned society for the AI age. The existing seal says it all: an engraved
owl of Athena above a circuit-board brain, ringed in laurel, sealed with a
human-robot handshake. Nashville is the Athens of the South (it has a
full-scale Parthenon); this group is its newest scholarly society, equal
parts 18th-century royal society and weeknight brewery meetup.

Three voice words: **engraved, lamplit, convivial.**

Physical object: a letterpress charter for a scientific society, printed in
gold ink on near-black paper, with one trace of circuit glow running
through the ornament.

The costume never gets in the way of the facts. Playful society language
("proceedings", "the society") is seasoning; dates, times, venues, and RSVP
links stay plain and instantly scannable.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dark-mode landing pages: purple gradients, glassmorphism,
  hero metrics, identical feature cards.
- "AI" visual clichés: neon blue brains, sparkles emoji energy, robotic
  sans-serif futurism.
- Corporate meetup templates (default Meetup.com page energy).
- Editorial-magazine affectation: this is heraldic and engraved, not a
  broadsheet.

## Strategic principles

1. The next event is the protagonist. A first-time visitor should know the
   date, venue, and how to RSVP within seconds.
2. All levels welcome, and it has to feel that way: warm, a little funny,
   zero gatekeeping.
3. The craft of the site is itself the pitch: "the people who made this take
   building things seriously, and they meet on Wednesdays."
